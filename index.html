About

I am a master’s student in the Department of Linguistics at Georgetown University, working with Professor Amir Zeldes and Professor Ethan Gotlieb Wilcox.

Research

Broadly, my research focuses on discourse and cognitive modeling. My previous and ongoing work covers the following research directions:

  • Language model evaluation
    • Designing benchmarks for discourse and linguistic understanding in large language models (LLMs).
  • Cognitive modeling of language comprehension
    • Examining differences between humans and language models in reading-time measures and neural signals.
    • Using interpretability methods to investigate model–human differences in language comprehension (e.g., in memory constraints).
    • Improving language models so their language comprehension more closely mimics human cognition.
